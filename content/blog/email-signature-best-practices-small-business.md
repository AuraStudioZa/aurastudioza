A good email signature answers three questions in two seconds: **who you are**, **how to reach you**, and **what you do**. For South African freelancers and small businesses, it is often the most-seen piece of branding after your website.

This article shares **practical tips**. It is not legal advice on POPIA or email marketing — if you send bulk campaigns or collect personal data through forms, review compliance separately.

## Keep the essentials visible

- **Name** and **role** (or company name)
- **One primary phone** — mobile with country code for SA clients
- **Email** — usually the address you are sending from
- **Website** — your .co.za or studio link
- Optional: **one** social or portfolio link, not five icons

Avoid clutter. Long disclaimers and quote blocks push contact details below the fold on mobile.

## Design for real mail clients

Gmail, Outlook, and mobile apps strip modern CSS. **Table-based HTML with inline styles** still renders most consistently. That is why our [free signature generator](/email-signature) outputs copy-paste HTML rather than a fancy layout that breaks in Outlook.

## Images and logos

- **Upload** a small PNG/JPEG if your mail host allows embedded images.
- **Hosted image URLs** work when you want a lighter signature — use a stable https link.
- Keep file size modest; huge logos slow loading and trigger spam filters.

## Mobile-first habits

Most business email is read on phones. Test your signature on mobile after pasting. Click-to-call `tel:` links help local clients. WhatsApp links are common in SA — only add one if you actually monitor that number.

## Optional corporate disclaimer

A one-line confidentiality note is common in large firms. For small businesses it is optional — accuracy and professionalism matter more than a long legal block recipients never read.

## Refresh when details change

Update your signature when you change number, bank details (never put full banking in a signature — use invoices for that), or branding. Stale signatures erode trust faster than no logo.
